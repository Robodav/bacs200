Shohini Ghose's Brilliant Quantum Computing TED Talk

Quantum physicist Shohini Ghose's presentation at TEDWomen 2018 shows the vast potential and amazing power quantum computing holds. Dr. Ghose first gives the live audience a better understanding of the difference between conventional and quantum computing by showing how they've already interacted with such a machine prior to the presentation. Unlike the binary "1's and 0's" nature of the layman's PC, a quantum computer is capable of calculations on a spectrum. There is no "either/or" in nature, and the quantum mechanics underlying the machine help it to understand that.

Quantum computing isn't just a fancy technology that can beat people at games of chance, though. Dr. Ghose goes on to show just how revolutionary it is by comparing binary machines to candles and quantum machines to light bulbs. This metaphor illustrates what a revolutionary bound quantum computers would be if they were integrated into everyday society. Ghose lists some of the advantages mankind could draw as follows:

  • Extremely sophisticated encryption through uncertainty principles
  • Advances in healthcare and medicine via molecule analysis
  • Teleportation of information without physical transmission (no, seriously!)

This all may sound like something out of a science fiction novel, but Dr. Ghose assures us that not only are these very real possibilities, some have even been demonstrated in controlled environments already. We are essentially on the brink of transforming society for the better through this new technology. Dr. Ghose perhaps puts it best: I don't see quantum physics as a tool just to build quantum computers. I see quantum computers as a way for us to probe the mysteries of nature, and reveal more about this hidden world outside of our experiences.
